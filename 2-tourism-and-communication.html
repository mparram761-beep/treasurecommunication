2 - Tourism and communication

Communication and tourism are closely connected because tourism depends on interaction between people from different countries, languages and cultures. In real-life situations, English is often used as an international language that allows tourists and local people to understand each other and communicate effectively.

In the tourism sector, communication is essential to give and receive information, ask for help, solve problems, offer services, explain cultural or historical aspects, and create positive social interactions.

Tourists use English in many everyday situations, for example: booking a hotel room, ordering food in a restaurant, asking for directions, visiting museums or monuments, shopping, or talking to tour guides.

At the same time, professionals working in tourism need communication skills to: welcome visitors,
provide clear instructions, answer questions politely, recommend places and activities, and help tourists feel comfortable and safe.

Good communication in tourism is not only about speaking correctly; it also involves active listening,
politeness, body language, cultural awareness, and the ability to adapt language to different people and situations.

Tourism also promotes intercultural communication because people learn about different traditions, lifestyles and perspectives. Through communication, tourists and local communities can exchange experiences and develop respect for cultural diversity.

For this reason, learning English through tourism-related situations helps students practise practical vocabulary and realistic conversations while developing confidence, cooperation and intercultural competence. It prepares them to communicate successfully in a globalised world where English is an important tool for real interaction.
